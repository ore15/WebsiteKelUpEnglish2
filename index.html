Part Of Speech

Pronoun Noun Verb Conjunction Interjection Adjective Adverb Preposition Article

Pronoun:

A pronoun is a word that is used instead of a noun or noun phrase. Example: he, she, it, i, you, we, they.


Noun:

A word that names a person, an animal, a place, or a thing. Noun falls into two categories, Common noun and Proper noun. Common noun refers to general things like planet, city, river, etc. Proper noun refers to specific things like earth, Indonesia, New York, etc.



Article

A pear. The beautiful perfromance. An amazing experience. The bolded word ar articles. They are noun signals. So article noun is The, a, an.


Verb

A verb is a word that shows action or being. Examples: eat, drink, walk, swim, etc.



Adjective(Kata Sifat)

An adjective is a word that tells us more abou a noun or a pronoun. Examples:He is my brother. She is very beautiful. More examples: old, big, happy, cold


Adverbs

And adverb is a wordthat tells us more about a noun or a pronoun. Examples: My brother writes clearly. The army fought bravely. Your friend called again. More examples: yesterday, near, slowly, often, extremely, etc.


Preposition

A word that shows the relationship of one word in a sentence. Examples: The pig is outside its cage. She is sleeping on her bed. More examples: in, on, at, above, below, onto, etc.


Conjunctions

A word that joins other words or parts of sentences together. it is a "bridge" word. Examples:it will rain or snow today. DAvide and Jennifer are brother and sister. More examples: For, and, nor, but, or, yet, so, etc.


Interjection

A word that express emotion. Like excitement, sadness,horror, etc. Examples: Oh!, Wow!, Well, Ouch!. Interjection usually come at the beginning of a sentence
